## Introduction

A great smile isn't just about aesthetics — it's a window into your overall health. The good news? Small, consistent habits make a huge difference. Here are five evidence-backed tips our team recommends to every patient.

---

## 1. Brush for the Right Amount of Time

Two minutes, twice a day — but most people brush for less than 45 seconds. Try setting a timer or using an electric toothbrush with a built-in timer. Use gentle, circular strokes at a **45-degree angle** to the gum line to avoid abrasion.

> **Pro tip:** Replace your toothbrush (or brush head) every 3 months, or sooner if the bristles look frayed.

---

## 2. Don't Skip Flossing

Flossing removes up to **40% of plaque** that your brush can't reach. If traditional floss feels awkward, try:

- Water flossers (great for braces or bridges)
- Interdental brushes
- Floss picks

Once a day before bedtime is enough to make a significant difference.

---

## 3. Watch Your Diet

Foods high in sugar and acid erode enamel over time. Swap out sugary sodas for water, and rinse your mouth after citrus fruits. Dairy products — cheese, yogurt, milk — are rich in **calcium and phosphates** that naturally strengthen enamel.

---

## 4. Stay Hydrated

Saliva is your mouth's natural defence system. It neutralises acids, washes away food particles, and delivers minerals to your teeth. Drinking **at least 8 glasses of water a day** keeps saliva production healthy and reduces bad breath.

---

## 5. Don't Miss Your Biannual Check-Up

Professional cleanings remove tartar that no amount of brushing can shift. Regular visits also let us catch cavities, gum disease, and even oral cancer in their earliest — most treatable — stages.
