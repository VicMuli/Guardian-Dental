## Episode Summary

Tooth sensitivity affects 1 in 8 adults, yet many people simply live with the pain rather than seeking help. In this episode we cover:

- **What causes sensitivity** — enamel erosion, exposed dentine, gum recession
- **Lifestyle triggers** — acidic foods, aggressive brushing, teeth grinding
- **Treatment options** — desensitising toothpaste, fluoride varnish, bonding agents
- **When to see a dentist** — red flags that indicate something more serious

## Key Takeaways

1. Use a **soft-bristled toothbrush** and gentle circular strokes
2. Wait 30 minutes after eating acidic foods before brushing
3. Ask your dentist about a **custom night guard** if you grind your teeth
4. Sensitivity toothpastes take 2–4 weeks to work — give them time
